Context
Teens are getting skincare advice from influencers with no medical background.

The insight behind GlowGuide came from a simple observation — beauty content is everywhere on social media, but almost none of it is safe, personalized, or age-appropriate for teenagers. GlowGuide fills that gap with dermatologist-backed recommendations behind an experience that actually feels made for a teenager.

Design Process
From concept to screen.

GlowGuide was designed entirely from research insights — starting with understanding how teenagers actually consume beauty content, through sketches, wireframes, and a fully polished hi-fi Figma prototype.

Sketching phase — These 4 screens map the product scanning feature flow: scan a barcode → view product details (ingredients, allergens, reviews) → check skin compatibility (with a clear NO indicator if it doesn't suit your skin type) → discover curated alternatives. This flow directly addresses the core problem of teenagers unknowingly buying products that are wrong for their skin.

Key decisions: using colour-coded headers (orange for scanner, pink for details, blue for compatibility, yellow for alternatives) to visually distinguish each step, and making the skin incompatibility message prominent and actionable rather than buried in fine print.

Low-fi wireframes — Moving into digital wireframes, I mapped every screen in the app: the full onboarding flow (launch → sign in/up → verify email → account setup), the home dashboard, the skin quiz and results, skincare and beauty sections with product info, and the social discover feed with posting and shade-match features.

Key changes from sketches: I clarified the tab navigation across all screens (Home, Skincare, Beauty, Discover), added the community story/post feature to the Discover section, and introduced the shade match tool as a tab within the Beauty section — addressing the virtual try-on requirement from user research.

High-fidelity screens — 12 redesigned screens across the full GlowGuide experience: onboarding with a full-bleed gradient flow, home dashboard, skin quiz with progress indicator, personalised results, skincare and beauty sections, discover feed, expert chat, shade match, and account settings.

The warm pink-to-lilac gradient system and Syne display typeface create a cohesive premium feel that is approachable enough for a first-time teen user while feeling polished enough to earn their trust on a health-adjacent topic.

Visual Design
GlowGuide's visual language needed to feel warm, trustworthy, and fun simultaneously — sophisticated enough for a teen who takes skincare seriously, approachable enough for a first-timer.
