Product
PantryPal
A smart kitchen companion app designed to help people cook from what they already have — tracking pantry inventory, alerting before expiration, and suggesting recipes matched to current ingredients.
Problem
Food Waste & Forgotten Groceries
Americans waste nearly $1,500 in groceries per year. People forget what they have, miss expiration dates, and order out rather than cooking what's already in their kitchen.
Solution
Cook From What You Have
PantryPal gives users a living inventory of their kitchen — with expiry alerts, ingredient-matched recipe suggestions, and a smart grocery list that fills only what's missing.

Context
Food waste is a $1,500/year problem for the average American household.

People don't waste food because they don't care — they waste it because there's no easy system to track what they have. User interviews revealed recipe discovery as the #1 pain point. PantryPal inverts the problem: start from your pantry, not from a recipe.

Sketching phase — Before touching Figma, I sketched all 12 screens across three core flows: pantry management, recipe discovery, and shopping list. The goal was to explore layout options and navigation patterns rapidly without getting caught up in visual details.

Key decisions: expiry-first hierarchy in the pantry, category-grid for recipe browsing, and a split "what I need / what I got" shopping list. These constraints shaped every wireframe and hi-fi screen that followed.

Low-fidelity wireframes — After validating the sketch concepts, I moved into Figma for digital lo-fi wireframes. This stage locked in the full navigation structure, content hierarchy, and all 14 screens before applying any visual styling.

Key additions from sketches: a dedicated launch screen, separate sign-up/sign-in flows, a filter panel for recipe discovery, and a "Choose from Pantry" flow that links grocery scanning directly to your pantry inventory.

High-fidelity screens — With layout validated through wireframes, I applied the full visual system — the hot pink brand colour, Playfair Display + DM Sans typography, expiry colour coding (red for expired, amber for expiring soon), and warm gradient backgrounds.

All 14 screens were designed in Figma at full fidelity, then connected into an interactive prototype covering every core user flow.

The prototype — The final Figma prototype connects all 14 screens with realistic interactions. A recruiter or usability tester can walk through the full user journey from launch screen through pantry management, recipe discovery, and grocery shopping — exactly as a real user would experience the app.

Visual Design
The visual direction for PantryPal was warm, approachable, and food-joyful — built on a hot pink primary that feels energetic without being aggressive, with a clean white system that keeps the focus on content.
